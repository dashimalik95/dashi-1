Root

Rooting Android is the same as jailbreaking on iPhone. When you root your Android, you gain access to all of the android operations. Rooting has many privileges like replacing the firmware, updating the OS, uninstalling system apps, installing unapproved apps, etc.

Given below are the different ways to root your android.


Note: Rooting an Android Device may void the warranty on the device or it may completely brick the device if not done properly. Root at your own risk, we do not take responsibility for your device. Only root if you know what you are doing. Study this topic thoroughly before rooting your Android Device.

CF Auto Root

CF Auto Root is the root for beginners and for the people who want to keep it close to the stock rom as it is compatible with the stock rom. If you are a beginner then you should choose this method because it is the quickest and easiest way for your first root. CF Auto Root installs SuperSU on your android device so that you can gain root access easily.


Framaroot

Framaroot is a root exploit app which is compatible with many devices. Its free of cost and enables the user to root the device without any Computer/Laptop. You can check compatibility of your device in framaroot supported devices list. If your device is compatible then download framaroot from here as it is not available in play store.



KingRoot

Kingroot is one click root tool for Android devices. It allows you to root your Android device in seconds. The advantage of KingRoot is that it does not trips the KNOX. It is compatible with almost all devices from Android 2.x to 5.0. KingRoot is free of cost. It is also available for PC if you want to root your android through your PC. You can download KingRoot from here.


360 Root

360 Root is a Chinese application for rooting android phone. It is compatible with Android 1.6 and higher. You can check compatibility of your device in 360 Root compatibility list.


Towelroot

Towelroot is a root exploit app that is free of cost and is compatible with a many devices. It was particularly developed for different models of Galaxy S5 but it is also compatible with many other devices. You can download towelroot from here.


Magisk Root

Magisk is just like the Chainfire SuperSU. It can root many devices. You can download Magisk from here.
